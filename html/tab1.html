Electric Energy

Is a form of energy that results from the presence and
movement of charged particles, such as electrons it
can be harnessed and used to power electrical devices
and systems. The unit of measurement for electric energy
is the kilowatt-hour (kWh).

Watt (physical unit)

The Watt is the SI unit of power, named after James Watt
the inventor of the steam engine. One Watt is defined as
the rate at which work is done when one ampere of electrical
electrical current flows through a potential difference of
one volt. In other words,one Watt is equal to one joule of
energy per second.The Watt is commonly used to express the
power of electrical devices, such as light bulbs, motors,
and heaters.

Linear regression

Linear regression is a statistical method used to model
the relationship between a dependent variable and one
or more independent variables. It assumes that the relationship
between the variables can be represented by a straight line,
and seeks to find the equation of that line that best fits
the observed data. The equation can then be used to make
predictions about the value of the dependent variable for
a given value of the independent variable. Linear regression
is widely used in various fields,ncluding economics, finance,
social sciences, engineering, and natural sciences.

Renewable energy

Renewable energy refers to energy that is derived from
renewable natural resources, such as sunlight, wind, rain,
tides, and geothermal heat, that can be replenished over time.
Unlike fossil fuels, which are finite and non-renewable, renewable
energy sources can be harnessed and used without depleting them.
Renewable energy is often considered as a clean and sustainable
alternative to conventional energy sources because it produces
little to no greenhouse gas emissions or other harmful pollutants.
Examples of renewable energy technologies include solar photovoltaic
systems, wind turbines, hydropower plants, and geothermal power plants.
